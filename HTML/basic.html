CATS

The cat, commonly referred to as the domestic cat or house cat, is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae. Recent advances in archaeology and genetics have shown that the domestication of the cat occurred in the Near East around 7500 BC.

Symphony of Meows: Exploring the Melodic World of Feline Vocalizations!

Meowing

Cats use meowing as a form of communication primarily with humans, expressing various needs and desires through different vocalizations.

Purring

Cats often purr when content, but may also purr in stressful situations, suggesting it serves as a self-soothing mechanism and a means of communication with both humans and other cats.

Purr-sonality Parade: Unveiling the Diverse Breeds of the Feline World!

Maine Coon

The Maine Coon is one of the largest domesticated cat breeds which originated in America, known for its large body, long tail and muscular build.

Persian

The Persian cat originated in Iran and are known for their luxurious long fur, typically round head and distinct flat faces. They have a calm and gentle temperament.

Sphynx

The Sphynx cat, a breed known for its hairless appearance and wrinkled skin, is highly distinctive. These cats, despite lacking fur, are extremely affectionate and form strong bonds with their owners.

Siamese

This breed originated in Thailand. With its striking beautiful blue eyes shaped like an almond and short, fine coat, the Siamese cat is known for its social and affectionate nature.

Bombay

With its sleek black colour coat and captivating copper or gold eyes, the cat having black hair is truly a sight to behold. Known for their friendly and affectionate nature, these black cats have medium-length legs.

Ragdoll

The Ragdoll Cat is a breed renowned for its calm and gentle temperament, making it an ideal pet. This feline has striking blue eyes and a luscious semi-longhair coat that comes in a variety of colours and patterns.
